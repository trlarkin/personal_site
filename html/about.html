My name is Tristan Larkin. Most of the time I am an over-caffeinated graduate student. Otherwise, I am hiking, camping, rock climbing, and ping ponging.

I am a computer science and physics graduate, and am continuing my education in physics at the University of New Mexico. I love learning about the clever ways clever people have discovered to understand our crazy universe. I get particular satisfaction from declarative/functional programming and symmetry. After this many programming and quantum mechanics classes I think it was inevitable that I would end up finding quantum computing completely fascinating! I love to teach and never fail to take advantage of an opportunity to introduce a new idea to a student or an interested friend.

I believe that programming is a beautiful way that we can express ourselves, which happens to also be the method we have to use to communicate with computers. I am a proponent of learning many programming languages, techniques, and paradigms to best expand your breath of knowledge. I had the opportunity to get into the weeds of Python while working on the OpenCSP project at Sandia National Laboratories, but my favorite take on programming is displayed by Haskell, which is a functional programming language that is "non-standard" in such a beautiful way. I also have dabbled with Julia, Pie, Scheme, C, Java, JS, and Go to varying degrees, and each of them provides a lens in which to view the problems you want to solve.
